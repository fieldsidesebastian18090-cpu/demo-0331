## About

**Pederin: The Confession** (《隐翅虫：告解》) is a 2D point-and-click narrative puzzle game set in a fictional Eastern European totalitarian state.

You play as **Anna Sergevna**, a writer who receives a mysterious timed letter from a girl named Aglaea — sent from apartment No. 605. Inside, you discover a former clinic turned residence, where every room holds fragments of a buried past: burn marks on a child's hands, sealed directives, a locked specimen cabinet, and the traces of a family torn apart by a secret bioweapon program.

## Features

- Multi-directional room exploration (front, left, right, back, floor, ceiling)
- Inventory-based puzzles with cross-room dependencies
- Reconstruction sequences — step into the past and witness key moments
- Environmental storytelling through objects, notes, and hidden details
- Bilingual support: English / Chinese (中文)

## Setting

**Morvask** — a post-war industrialized authoritarian state. The national creed, "The Accord," demands the individual serve the collective, and the collective serve the State. Beneath the surface, the **Fourth Directorate (Dir-IV)** runs a secret biochemical weapons program. The apartment you've entered was once part of it.

## Demo Content

This demo covers:
- **Prologue**: The letter, the entrance, the corridor
- **Chapter 1**: Four rooms (Living Room, Kitchen, Bedroom, Storage), interconnected puzzles, one reconstruction sequence
- Approximately 30–45 minutes of gameplay
